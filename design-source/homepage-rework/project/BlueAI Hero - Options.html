Hero — Design Review

Three directions for the agent hero.

All three share the brand, layout grid, and the same three live-agent animations (Career, Creator, Finance). They differ in how the agents are showcased — open any to see it run. We've flagged the strongest for UX and click-through below.

1

Three Cards

Centered headline and CTA, with three equal agent cards that play side by side.

✓Shows breadth instantly — users grasp there are three distinct agents in one look.
✕Attention splits three ways. Each demo is small and hard to read, and the CTA competes with three cards — no single, clear thing to do.
Open
2

Stage + Thumbnails (original)

Centered headline and CTA stacked on top, with one big agent stage and a thumbnail rail below.

✓One large, legible demo — the agent's process is easy to follow at full size.
✕Proof sits below the fold. The stacked headline pushes the live demo down, and on shorter screens the CTA scrolls out of view.
Open
3 ★ Recommended

Stage + Thumbnails

Message on the left; on the right the agent thumbnails sit above one dominant stage that plays in place.

✓Value + proof on one screen. Headline, CTA and a large live demo are visible together at the top — and the Download button never leaves the viewport.
✓One agent at a time stays readable, and the thumbnails make the next agent an obvious tap — interaction that pulls users in.
✕Only one agent shows at once — the other two are a tap (or a 5s cycle) away.
